CellCut: Unsupervised Cellular Image Segmentation through Self-Supervision

Markus Markus*, Neehar Kondapaneni*, Aditya Mehta, Andrew Zabelo, Pietro Perona
California Institute of Technology
CVPR 2025
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Code

Abstract

Cells are essential components of biological systems, and accurately segmenting them is crucial for analyzing microscopy images. While deep learning has improved cell segmentation, it relies on large annotated datasets, which are often unavailable for new imaging conditions. Variations in cell size, shape, and imaging methods usually require new training data, and existing models struggle with out-of-distribution cells. To address this, we propose a novel unsupervised/semi-supervised method leveraging self-supervised models to identify patch-level similarities in cell images. Using these similarities, our method iteratively creates cell masks, builds an initial training set, and improves segmentation through self-training. This approach reduces the need for manual annotations, enabling more effective high-throughput image analysis and broader applicability of segmentation models. We show that we can fine-tune state-of-the-art cell segmentation models on some out-of-distribution datasets and achieve close to fully supervised performance with only 20 manually annotated cells.
